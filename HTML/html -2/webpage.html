Biography

Born on June 28, 1971, in Pretoria, South Africa, Elon Musk is a South African-Canadian entrepreneur, inventor, and business magnate. He became a millionaire by age 31 after selling PayPal, a digital payment service, for over $1 billion. Musk’s parents divorced when he was 10, and he moved to Canada at 17 to attend Queen’s University. He earned a degree in economics and physics in 1992. His interest in computers and software led him to teach himself programming and create his first software game, Blastar, at 12. Elon Musk’s entrepreneurial spirit, innovative thinking, and vision for a sustainable future have made him a global icon and a driving force in shaping the future of technology, transportation, and energy.

Highlights

  1. Co-founded PayPal (1999) and sold it to eBay for $1.5 billion
  2. Founded SpaceX (2002) to develop commercial space travel and was awarded a NASA contract to transport cargo to the International Space Station
  3. Co-founded Tesla, Inc. (2004) and became its CEO, leading the electric vehicle and clean energy revolution
  4. Founded The Boring Company (2016) to develop high-speed transportation systems
  5. Co-founded Neuralink (2016) and OpenAI (2015) to advance artificial intelligence and neuroscience
  6. Became CEO of Twitter (2022) and has been a vocal supporter of Donald Trump and his policies
